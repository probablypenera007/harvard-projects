Back Pain and Back Injury


ER

If: Back pain with fever , numbness, weakness, bladder or bowel incontinence (loss of control) or any recent injury or trauma, especially in the elderly


Urgent Care or Primary Care

If: A recent increase in back pain that does not subside with over-the-counter medication, pain associated with nausea and/or vomiting, pain that becomes severe with specific movements, such as while coughing, bending forward or backward, or during twisting movements, recent onset of back stiffness.
